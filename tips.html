Useful Tips

Below are some tips and hints we have pulled together to help give you some ideas of how you can have an impact in your daily life. Even if you only apply one or two items evey little bit makes a difference!

Shopping


  • Remember your reuseable shopping bag
  • Where possible, buy loose fruit and vegetables to reduce plastic waste
  • Try and buy organic food which has not been subject to pesticide usage
  • For comestics, try brands that are not tested on animals
  • Even better, try making your own cosmetics and cleaning products!
  • Ditch the wipes! Use old cloths, biodegradable wipes (or the good old Irish mammy trick of spitting on a tissue!)
  • Try and buy biodegradable or compostable for example bin bags, tea bags etc.
  • Try your local Farmer's market or a fruit and vegetable delivery company for fresh often organic and locally sourced food
  • Where possible, buy refillable containers for detergents etc. Find a local eco-store that allows you to buy on basis of quantity you need
  • Try bamboo brushes instead of plastic toothbrushes
  • Buy recycled paper products such as kitchen paper, toilet paper, notebooks etc.
  • Reduce your red meat consumption for even a few meals or days a week

Home


  • When you need to replace your light bulbs, try and get energy efficient or LED bulbs
  • Switch off appliances at the plug to reduce energy consumption
  • Conserve water but using dishwashers and washing machines when full, reducing shower times and turning off the tap when brushing your teeth
  • Turn your thermostat down a degree or two
  • Use washable cotton pads instead of disposable and paper cotton buds instead of plastic
  • When changing your appliances, go for A rated energy efficient appliances
  • Know yourt recycling and what goes in what bin. Check out this handy guide from Panda
  • Reduce your usage of cling film: try beeswax container covers, aluminium foil or even a resusable shower hat to cover bowls
  • Try to rehome or repurpose items rather than sending to landfill, most charity shops will gladly welcome what you no longer need
  • Use left over food and get creative to plan dishes around what is left in the fridge
  • Look at switching to a renewable energy source for your home such as wind or solar energy
  • Explore better home insulation and what grants may be available to you to improve your BER rating

Out & About


  • Walk, cycle or take public transport where you can to reduce your car usage
  • Try and reduce your air travel; consider more staycations (We know, we know we've just had 2 years of COVID!)
  • Try and car-share for school runs or work
  • Wash your car less often or use grey-water instead of the hose
  • When changing your car, look into a hybrid or electric vehicle
  • When travelling, apply to same items above that you do at home re energy and water usage
  • When staying in hotels, avoid using the cute teeny tiny mini toiletries they give you (unless you are just holding them to feel like a giant - that's totally fine!)
  • Bring a reusble coffee cup/bottle/cutlery with you to save on disposable cups and plastic
  • Use e-tickets and apps for tickets where possible
  • Change that smartphone less frequently (That 1980's brick is gonna come right back into fashion - we know it!)
  • Avoid or reseach animal attractions (elephant rides, wild cat photos), animal souvenirs (ivory, fur etc) when you are on holidays as these are often supporting illegal or unsavoury practices
  • If you have a dog, (you lucky duck!) bring biodegradable poo bags for use when out walking

Clothes


  • Reduce the amount of clothes you buy, buy fewer good quality clothes that will last
  • Check out local vintage and charity shops for one-off items
  • When you are finished with clothes, give them a new home through your local charity shop or local clothes recycling bank
  • Reduce how frequently you wash your clothes and dont just wash after every wear unless required
  • Look into buying more natural fibres than polyester and nylon such as organic cottons, bamboo etc
  • Buy from brands who care, who demonstrate that they have an eco-friendly and/or sustainability policy through the fabrics they use, their suppy chain, their packaging etc
  • Try line drying instead of using the tumble dryer (Yes, we know we live in Ireland but sometimes it is sunny...well dry at least...the wind can be great for drying clothes!)
  • Use a micro-fiber catcher in your washing machine
  • Reduce dry-cleaning clothes
  • Reuse old clothes as cloths for use around the house in cleaing, for cleaning the car, windows etc.

Garden


  • Try and minimise your use of the garden hose and sprinklers, use grey water from the house where possible
  • Avoid using chemicals and pestcides in the garden, explore natural pesticides
  • Grow some of your own fruit, veg, herbs even if you only have a small garden or balcony
  • Build or buy an insect hotel for the garden
  • Make your garden animal friendly; set up bird feeders, hedgehog passages (and if you have hedgehogs call us we love those little guys!)
  • Install a water butt to give you a constant supply of water for usage in the garden (An upside to living in Ireland!)
  • If you have the space, plant some native trees
  • Get creative; reuse old wooden pallets etc to create garden furniture, plant holders and garden features
  • Start your own composting
  • If you don't have a garden, look into a local allotment to grow veg and get to know other members of the community

Work & Business


  • Use refillable or recyclable printer toner and inks
  • Reduce paper usage; minimise printing and if printing, print on both sides
  • Provide recycling facilities for staff
  • Sign up to get your statememts online
  • Work online through document sharing with team members
  • Don't send junk mail - people will like you for it!
  • For events, minimise the use of balloons
  • As a company, get involved in the community, participate in events, provide sponsorship, encourage employees to get invovled
  • Draft policies for your company around sustainability and what your company's principles are in this area. Let your customers know you care!
  • Don't change laptops, business phones and electronic equipment quite as regularly
